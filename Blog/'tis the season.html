‘TIS THE SEASON TO BE GIVING

‘TIS THE SEASON TO BE GIVING

  • Christmas, Crowdfunding, donate, orphans
  • December 23, 2020

On the 25th of December every year, the world comes together to celebrate the birth of Christ. To do that, they extend a giving hand to those in need and wrap their love in a shiny box placed under the tree.
Despite being a religious holiday meant for Christians, the Christmas spirit is felt by all. But celebrating a festival comes with a certain amount of privilege that isn’t shared by everyone.
According to the Orphans Report of 2020, approximately 31 million Indians are orphans, which means that millions of children miss out on the opportunity to celebrate the festival of love and lights every year. Except, it doesn’t have to be that way.

HERE IS A LIST OF THINGS YOU CAN DO TO GIVE THE ORPHANS A CHANCE TO CELEBRATE ALONG WITH YOU

  1. Be Santa Claus

    2. If you’re reading this, you should know that Santa does not live in the sky and come out every December (unless you’re under 7 years old then this is a lie to confuse the adults, keep a secret for me, won’t you?)
    But that does not mean that Santa does not exist, in fact, there are many. And you can be one too. Here is how:
    – Contact your local orphanage and ask them for their wish-lists or letters to Santa.
    – Send money to them directly for them to be able to buy the presents or do it yourself, and leave a sweet note from Santa.
    – You could also visit the orphanage on Christmas in your favorite Santa costume and distribute presents of your choosing.

  2. Donate

    3. There are hundreds of websites dedicated to helping you make a donation from your bed, wrapped in the new blanket you got as a present. But before you spend your money, you’re going to want to do some research, and rightfully so.
    Helping Panda is an up-and-coming crowdfunding website designed to make your research easy. All campaigns are categorized carefully to help you find what cause you want to donate to, with background information and contact details to clear any doubts you have.

  3. Gift A Long-Term Present

    4. Christmas presents don’t all have to be for immediate consumption. If you’re able you can invest in the future of the child(ren) by paying for their annual medical check-up or a dentist appointment – basic health care is for everyone.
    Other long-term presents could be paying for education. You could pay the full amount (of annual fees) from personal savings or by raising money through fundraisers or setting aside a percentage of proceeds from sales if you have a successful or well-known business.
    Give what you can this Christmas. Make a difference in an orphan’s life.
