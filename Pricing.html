Funding Method

All or Nothing

These are campaigns that need to reach their funding goal to become a reality. This is why the campaign has to reach its goal before its end date for the campaign owner to get all the collected funds. If the campaign fails to reach its goal, then the funds are returned back to the funders.
Example: Construction of a educational institute, organising an event, release of a music album, production of a film etc.

Keep what you get

These are campaigns that can succeed in a limited way with whatever amount they are able to collect. Here the campaign owners gets to keep whatever has been collected irrespective of whether the campaign reached its target or not.
Example: pre-orders for a new product, disaster relief efforts, urgent funds for a medical cause, money needed to run NGOâ€™s, vocational training of students etc.
